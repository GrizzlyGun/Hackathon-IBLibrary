The Caged Birds:

Overview & Summary

In the world-renowned play 'Macbeth', Act 1 Scene 1 emphasizes the basis of the play while setting the future course of the story. In the story, it starts off as eerie thunder and lightning disperse across a plane in Scotland. In this ominous setting, three witches are seen planning to meet on the heath after Macbeth's battle in order to confront them. Though in a sudden blink of an eye the ladies vanquish and progress to the next scene. In the story ‘Macbeth’ written by William Shakespeare, several techniques are incorporated to set the storyline through Act 1 Scene 1. While impacting the audience through creating the background and overview of the situation for the story, the readers will be greatly affected by the plot of the story. Through the ominous setting, sinister mood, and use of wordplay, this scene of Macbeth can be impactful for the audience while setting the basis of the story.

Analysis

  1. Setting

      • The second Witch mentions “Upon the Heath”

  2. Audience & Purpose
    • The audience and purpose can be considered for general society. Primarily, this is because after encountering this historical gem, Macbeth embodies the concepts of protagonists, antagonists, morals, etc. While pioneering the ages of modern-day storytelling, Macbeth should be read by everyone.
  3. Themes
    • Throughout act 1 scene 1, the theme would be something along the lines of being sinful and deceitful. This is mainly due to the actions on behalf of the witches through their plotting and demonic acts.
  4. Tone & Mood
    • The tone is ominous, and the mood is suspenseful.
    • Example:
      • All the witches exclaim “Fair is foul, and foul is fair; Hover through the fog and filthy air.
